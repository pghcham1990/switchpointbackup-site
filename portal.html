PHASE 1 — THE APPROACH


    

      
YOUR OPENER — memorize this

      
"Hi, I help businesses like yours stay online when your internet goes down. If your internet dropped right now, could you still run credit cards and pull up patient records?"

      
⏱ Then SHUT UP. Let them answer. The pause is where the sale starts.

    


    
THEY RESPOND WITH...


    

      

        🟢
        "Yeah we've had issues with that..."
        ›
      

      

        

          
🔥 HOT LEAD — they're selling themselves

          
Let them talk. Nod. Say "wow" and "how long were you down?" Ask: "What did that cost you that day?" Let them quantify their own pain.

          
Then: "That's exactly what we prevent. We install a small backup device — when your internet drops, it switches you to LTE automatically in about 10 seconds. Your staff doesn't touch anything."

          
→ Go to Closing

        

      

    


    

      

        🟡
        "Not really / we've been fine"
        ›
      

      

        

          
PLANT THE SEED

          
"That's great — honestly most of my customers said the same thing right up until it happened. Comcast doesn't warn you. I'll leave you my card. When it does go down and they tell you 4-6 hours, give me a call. I can usually have you protected within a week."

          
Leave the card. Move on. Don't oversell. The outage will do your selling for you.

        

      

    


    

      

        🟡
        "Who are you with?" / "What company?"
        ›
      

      

        

          
IDENTITY FRAME

          
"SwitchPoint Backup — we're a local managed service. I'm Scott, I'm based in Bridgeville. We install and monitor backup internet devices for businesses in the area. Think of it like a generator for your internet — you don't replace the electric company, but you want the lights on when the power goes out."

          
Key: "Local" and "I'm Scott" builds trust. You're not Comcast. You're a person.

        

      

    


    

      

        🔴
        "Not interested" / "We're good"
        ›
      

      

        

          
DON'T PUSH — PLANT & LEAVE

          
"Totally understand. Here's my card in case anything changes — when the internet goes down and Comcast says 4-6 hours, we're the guys who keep you running. Have a great day."

          
Smile. Leave. 90 seconds total. You just planted a time bomb. The next outage, they'll look at your card.

        

      

    

  


  
  

    
PHASE 2 — OBJECTION HANDLING


    

      

        🛡️
        "We already have Comcast / internet"
        ›
      

      

        

          
REFRAME — you're not replacing, you're backing up

          
"Oh I'm not replacing Comcast — I love Comcast. They're your power company. I'm the generator. When they go down — and they will — I keep you running until they come back. Your staff doesn't even notice."

          
🧠 Why this works: The generator analogy instantly reframes from competitor to insurance. Everyone understands generators. Nobody argues with having a generator.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "We already have a backup"
        ›
      

      

        

          
GET CURIOUS — ask what it is

          
"Oh nice — what are you using?"

        

        

          

            ↳
            "Second Comcast / cable line"
            ›
          

          

            

              
"Both lines come in on the same cable from the same pole. If a truck hits that pole or Comcast has an area outage, both go down at the same time. We use LTE — completely different network, different infrastructure. Doesn't matter what happens to the cable."

              
🧠 Why this works: Single point of failure is a concept they immediately understand. You just made their "backup" feel like a liability.

            

          

        

        

          

            ↳
            "Our IT guy set something up"
            ›
          

          

            

              
"Does it switch over automatically, or does someone have to do it manually? And who's monitoring it to make sure the backup is actually working right now?"

              
Usually nobody is. "That's the other half of what we do — I monitor your connection 24/7. If anything hiccups, I know before you do."

              
🧠 Why this works: Two killer questions that expose the gap. Manual = downtime. No monitoring = false sense of security. You just created doubt without attacking their IT guy.

            

          

        

        

          

            ↳
            "We have a hotspot"
            ›
          

          

            

              
"So when the internet goes down, someone has to dig out the hotspot, turn it on, figure out the password, reconnect every device... and your staff is standing around for 20 minutes. Our device is already connected, already configured, and switches in 10 seconds with nobody touching anything."

              
🧠 Why this works: Paint the chaos picture. They've lived it. Make them feel the frustration of the manual process, then contrast with automatic.

            

          

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "How much does it cost?"
        ›
      

      

        

          
💰 BUYING SIGNAL — they're interested

          
"$99 a month. That includes the device, the LTE service, installation, and 24/7 monitoring. No contract beyond month-to-month."

          
If they flinch: "Think about what one hour of downtime costs you. If you've got four chairs running and nobody can check in patients or run cards — that's $1,000+ you'll never get back. $99 is insurance for that."

          
If they don't flinch: They're ready. Go to Closing.

          
🧠 Why this works: Loss framing. Don't justify $99/month. Make them calculate what downtime costs. $99 becomes invisible against a $1,000 loss.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "I need to talk to my partner / boss / IT"
        ›
      

      

        

          
DON'T FIGHT IT — arm them to sell for you

          
"Absolutely — can I leave you a one-pager that explains it? That way when you talk to them, you have the details in front of you. What's the best email to send it to?"

          
Get the email. Now you have a warm lead with permission to follow up. Send the proposal same day.

          
🧠 Why this works: "Let me leave you something" is non-threatening. Getting the email converts a maybe into a pipeline prospect with a follow-up date.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "We can't afford it right now"
        ›
      

      

        

          
REFRAME — it's cheaper than not having it

          
"I get it. Here's the thing though — you can't afford $99 a month, but can you afford to lose an afternoon of revenue? Last month a dental office in Washington had their internet down for 6 hours. They sent patients home. That's $5,000+ they'll never recover."

          
"$99 isn't an expense — it's insurance. It's less than what you'd spend replacing one patient visit you lost to an outage."

          
🧠 Why this works: Reframing cost as savings. The $99 isn't going OUT — it's preventing a much larger number from disappearing. Prospect theory: people fear losses 2x more than they value equivalent gains.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "Our internet never goes down"
        ›
      

      

        

          
CERTAINTY ATTACK

          
"That's awesome — you're lucky. Can I ask, do you have insurance on the building?"

          
They say yes.

          
"Ever had a fire?"

          
They say no.

          
"That's exactly what this is. You don't buy insurance because something bad happened — you buy it because something bad could happen. And when it does, you're glad you have it."

          
🧠 Why this works: The insurance analogy is inarguable. Nobody cancels fire insurance because they haven't had a fire. You just made their logic work against their objection.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "Send me some info / email me"
        ›
      

      

        

          
⚠️ BRUSH-OFF — but still get the email

          
"Sure — what's the best email? I'll send over a quick one-pager."

          
Get the email. Send same day. Follow up in 3 days.

          
This is often a polite no. That's fine. But 20% of "send me info" people convert after they actually read it and then experience an outage. Plant the seed.

          
🧠 Why this works: Don't fight the brush-off. Comply and extract the email. You've turned a cold no into a warm lead in your CRM with a follow-up date. That's a win.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "What happens if LTE goes down too?"
        ›
      

      

        

          
SMART QUESTION — respect it

          
"Great question. The Peplink device supports multiple LTE carriers — if one tower is down, it can fail to another. But honestly, the odds of your cable AND the LTE network going down at the exact same time are almost zero. Different infrastructure, different companies, different technology. It's like your electricity and your gas going out simultaneously."

          
🧠 Why this works: This person is technical and already thinking about buying. Don't be defensive. Praise the question, give the honest answer, then reduce the perceived risk.

        

      

    


    

      

        🛡️
        "Is there a contract?"
        ›
      

      

        

          
💰 ANOTHER BUYING SIGNAL

          
"Month to month. No long-term contract. If you don't love it, you call me and we pick up the equipment. But honestly, nobody's ever cancelled — once it saves you from one outage, you'll never go back."

          
🧠 Why this works: Risk removal. Month-to-month eliminates the commitment fear. "Nobody's ever cancelled" is social proof. They're asking logistical questions because they're mentally buying.

        

      

    

  


  
  

    
PHASE 3 — CLOSING


    

      
THE ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE

      
"So here's what we'd do — I come out, install the device next to your router, takes about 10 minutes. I test everything, make sure it fails over properly. Then I monitor it from my end 24/7. You'll never think about it again until the day your internet goes down and your staff doesn't even notice. What day works better for a quick install — this week or next?"

      
Don't ask "would you like to move forward?" — ask WHEN. Assume the yes.

    


    
THEY SAY...


    

      

        🟢
        "Sure, let's do it" / picks a day
        ›
      

      

        

          
🎉 YOU JUST CLOSED YOUR FIRST CUSTOMER

          
Get these right now:

          
1. Their name and email (for the service agreement)

          2. Best phone number

          3. Confirm the address

          4. Preferred install date/time

          
"I'll email you a simple service agreement today. Once you sign it, I'll schedule the install. The whole thing takes about 10 minutes and your team won't have any downtime."

          
Send the agreement SAME DAY. Do not wait.

        

      

    


    

      

        🟡
        "Let me think about it"
        ›
      

      

        

          
LOCK THE FOLLOW-UP

          
"Absolutely — it's a smart decision to think it over. Can I follow up with you next week? What's the best day — Tuesday or Thursday?"

          
Get a specific day. Not "I'll call you sometime." A day. Put it in your CRM the second you walk out.

          
🧠 Why this works: The either/or question ("Tuesday or Thursday?") is a classic — both options are a yes. You've turned "let me think about it" into a scheduled follow-up.

        

      

    


    

      

        🟡
        "What's the next step?"
        ›
      

      

        

          
💰 THEY'RE IN — just tell them what to do

          
"Simple — I'll email you a one-page service agreement today. You sign it electronically, takes 2 minutes. Then I schedule the install. I come out, put the device next to your router, configure everything, test the failover, and you're protected. Whole thing takes less time than a lunch break."

          
Make it sound effortless. Because it is.

        

      

    

  


  
  

    
SALES PSYCHOLOGY — your weapons


    

      
🧠 LOSS AVERSION

      
People fear losing $100 twice as much as they enjoy gaining $100. Never sell what they'll gain — sell what they'll lose without it.

      
Use: "What does one hour of downtime cost you?" — make them calculate the loss, not the price.

    


    

      
🧠 THE GENERATOR ANALOGY

      
Your most powerful reframe. Instantly shifts from "competitor" to "insurance." Nobody argues with having a generator. Nobody cancels fire insurance because they haven't had a fire.

      
Use: Any time they say "we already have internet." You're not replacing it — you're the backup.

    


    

      
🧠 SOCIAL PROOF

      
People follow what others do. Reference other businesses casually — "a dental office in Washington" or "a few practices in the area." Even without names, it signals you're not their first.

      
Use: "Most of my customers said the same thing right up until it happened."

    


    

      
🧠 ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE

      
Never ask "would you like to do this?" Ask WHEN. "This week or next?" assumes the yes and makes the decision about timing, not commitment.

      
Use: "What day works better for a quick install?"

    


    

      
🧠 EITHER/OR TECHNIQUE

      
Give two options that both equal yes. "Tuesday or Thursday?" Not "should I call you?" Both answers move forward.

      
Use: Follow-ups, install scheduling, any decision point.

    


    

      
🧠 PAIN AMPLIFICATION

      
When they mention a past outage, don't rush to your solution. Ask follow-up questions. "How long were you down? What happened to your patients? Could you process cards?" Let the pain build. THEY sell themselves.

      
Use: Any time they mention past internet issues. Dig deeper before pitching.

    


    

      
🧠 RISK REMOVAL

      
"Month to month, no contract, cancel any time." Eliminates the commitment fear. Once the device saves them once, they'll never cancel. You know this — let the product prove itself.

      
Use: Price/contract objections. Remove every barrier to yes.

    


    

      
🧠 THE 90-SECOND RULE

      
If the conversation isn't going anywhere, leave in 90 seconds. Leave a card. Don't beg. Desperation kills deals. A confident exit with a card plants a seed that grows every time their internet flickers.

      
Use: Any "not interested" — smile, card, gone. Next door.

📋 PA LLC Tax Notes (Single-Member, Pass-Through)

    
SwitchPoint Backup LLC is a single-member PA LLC taxed as a sole proprietorship. All income/expenses flow to your personal 1040 via Schedule C. PA flat income tax rate is 3.07%. Federal self-employment tax is 15.3% (first $168,600 for 2026). QBI deduction is 23% for 2026 under OBBBA. Partner cash bonuses under $600/year per person do NOT require a 1099 — but they ARE still deductible business expenses. Keep records.

    
⚠️ Meals are only 50% deductible. Gifts capped at $25/person/year. Vehicle: track mileage at $0.725/mile for 2026 OR actual expenses. Section 179 lets you deduct equipment immediately (up to $2.5M). 100% bonus depreciation restored for 2026.

    
This is not tax advice. Consult your accountant. This tool is a record-keeping aid for organizing expenses by Schedule C category.
