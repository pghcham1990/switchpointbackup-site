What SwitchPoint is

SwitchPoint is a managed internet failover service. We deploy a device that sits above your existing carrier and keeps your business online when your primary ISP fails by switching to cellular automatically.

1

Keep your current internet

No provider change. Your ISP stays exactly as it is. We add failover on top.

2

Backup activates only during outages

LTE is idle during normal operation. It turns on when the primary link fails and turns off when it returns.

3

Managed device on site

We install, configure, monitor, and support the failover equipment and SIM as one managed system.

Why this exists: When a business loses internet, work stops. Payments can fail, phones can drop, and teams lose access to cloud tools. A controlled LTE failover system reduces downtime without turning into a full IT project.

How it works

1
Install the SwitchPoint device We place the device between your internet handoff (modem or fiber) and your network equipment.
2
Set outage detection and failover rules When the primary connection fails, the device automatically routes outbound traffic over LTE.
3
Test it in front of you We simulate an outage so you see it switch to LTE and then recover back to the primary link.
4
Monitor, alert, and support We monitor connectivity and usage through a cloud portal and support the failover device.
Power outage note: LTE failover protects against ISP outages while your building has power. If you want brief power outage coverage, ask about adding a small UPS for the modem and failover device.

Hard caps and approval-based add-ons

Controls that keep costs predictable

The backup connection is intentionally constrained. This is not a second full time internet circuit. The goal is continuity during outages with predictable behavior and clear approval steps.

A

Monthly data cap example

Example cap: 1 GB per month for failover usage. This is typically enough to keep a small team operational during a short outage while the ISP restores service.

B

Hard stop, no surprises

When the LTE cap is reached, the backup connection stops. There are no automatic overages. You choose whether to add more continuity or wait for the primary link to return.

C

Add more only with approval

If the outage continues and you want additional LTE capacity, we can add it on demand. We do not add usage or charges without explicit authorization.

What we manage: the failover device, the SIM, monitoring, alerts, and the policies that control usage. What we do not do: we do not replace your ISP and we do not provide primary internet service.

FAQ

Are you an internet provider

No. SwitchPoint is not an ISP, carrier, or utility. Your primary internet service remains with your existing provider. We install and manage a failover device that switches to LTE only when the primary link fails.

Do I need a new internet provider

No. We keep your current provider. SwitchPoint sits above your existing connection and provides continuity during outages.

Do I need a static IP

Usually no. This service is designed primarily for outbound business continuity during outages. If your environment requires inbound access or special routing, we can review it as a higher tier deployment.

What happens when the LTE cap is used up

The LTE backup stops at the cap to prevent unexpected costs. At that point you can either wait for the primary ISP to return, or authorize additional LTE capacity to stay online during the outage.

Will it work during a power outage

If the building has no power, your network equipment will shut down. LTE failover protects against ISP outages while the building has power. For brief power outages, a small UPS can keep the modem and SwitchPoint device running.

How fast is failover

Most environments fail over within about 10 to 30 seconds. We test failover during installation.
